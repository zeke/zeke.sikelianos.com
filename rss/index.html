What is RSS?

RSS stands for Really Simple Syndication.

It's a standardized way for websites to publish their content in a structured format, and for consumers of that content to easily subscribe to updates.

So, instead of manually checking your favorite websites for updates (or relying on algorithms to serve you a feed of curated content), you use an RSS reader and it brings new posts to you as they're published.

How to Subscribe

You can use RSS without installing anything by using an email-to-RSS service or a web-based reader.

Option 1: Get updates by email (no app install)

  • Blogtrottr: paste the feed URL, enter your email, choose a schedule (immediate or digest).
  • follow.it: subscribe to the feed and choose email delivery (immediate, daily, or weekly).

Option 2: Read in a browser (no app install)

  • Feedly (web + mobile)
  • Inoreader (web + mobile)
  • NewsBlur (web + mobile)

Option 3: Apps

  • NetNewsWire (Mac + iOS)
  • Reeder (Mac + iOS)
